Medical Device · NEU London

NerveSense

A real-time surgical proximity device that helps surgeons detect and avoid peripheral nerves during incision. The device stimulates tissue with a TENS unit and measures the nerve's electrical response through a scalpel-mounted electrode to estimate distance.

  • Custom PCB designed in Altium with INA333 biomedical amplifier and bandpass filter
  • AD620 amplifier module (100× gain) for signal amplification
  • Raspberry Pi Pico reads ADC, runs exponential distance model (R²=0.93)
  • 20×4 I2C LCD displays real-time proximity — detects nerves up to 5cm away (±0.5cm)
  • AgCl-coated silver electrodes to minimize capacitive interference
  • CAD-designed 3D-printed enclosure and scalpel probe assembly
